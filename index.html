Armour↑

Due to the remarkable weight, size and cost of examples of mecha within fiction, near-impervious amounts of armour are necessary. However, due to the inefficiencies which come from working with such a scale, the metals used are themselves fictional.

Some properties of these sorts of armour include high strength, low weight, invisibility to radar, electrical non-conductivity, and unique electromagnetic properties in response to weaponry of the setting. This defensive capacity generally means that the only way to damage or destroy a mecha is with weapons so large that only another mecha can carry and use them in combat.

If a modern-day material is used, it will typically be some sort of titanium alloy, but with better properties than what are currently possible today.

Cockpit↑

The cockpit is the most important location as it's where the pilot controls the suit. In most franchises the cockpit will take the fewest liberties technology-wise and rely mostly on elements which exist today. A typical depiction would simply have a chair surrounded with buttons, switches, throttles and a handful of displays. Sometimes large screen or projection-based displays are used, in less-common examples the display is within the equivalent of a current-day virtual reality headset.

While the interface the pilot uses is a very straight-forward and simple implementation, sci-fi and creative liberties come in with how the machine responds to the pilot’s input, as it mostly manages its own balance and coordination through complex movements initiated through minimal effort on the pilot’s part.

Weapons↑

Weaponry used falls largely into two categories: weapons which are much larger versions of what we currently have today, and weapons which rely on some form of sci-fi technology

Large conventional weapons include things such as machine guns, missiles, artillery and other ballistic weaponry. Sci-Fi weaponry includes laser, beam and plasma guns, high frequency or high heat melee weapons and giant laser swords. In rare occurrences, a melee weapon will be something completely normal with no real technology behind it, such as just a giant sword or a giant club.

Usually a setting has an over-abundance of jamming and continuous electromagnetic pulse technologies which eliminate the use of any guided weapons and any electronics not within a heavily shielded housing. Because of this, heavily armoured machines which can travel to a very close range where weapons with no targeting or guidance systems can be used effectively are a necessity.

Reactor and Thrusters↑

The power source typically used when described will be some sort of miniaturized nuclear reactor. Some franchises such as Gundam cite the fusion reaction between helium-3 and heavy hydrogen, as it’s a high-energy reaction which produces no neutron radiation. Due to the scarcity of helium-3 on earth, at least within the massive industrial scale used in these settings, space-based production from gas giants such as Jupiter becomes a necessary form of trade and infrastructure.

Thrusters will either be powered directly by the reactor or will use some sort of conventional rocket fuel. Sometimes massive sets are used with space-combat models for increased mobility and power output.

It isn’t uncommon for underlying technologies and engineering to be glossed over at first and then described at a later time for how they actually work.
